Directed Situations

  • Overview
  • Examples
  • Tips and Tricks
  • Exercises
  • Resources

Directed Situations are short scenarios or situations in which you are required to provide a one sentence response in Spanish.

For example, you are given the following scenario:

You are going to be late for an activity. Send a message to the organiser giving two reasons for your tardiness.

In the example, you are required to do two things:

  • Indicate that you will be late, and,
  • State two reasons why you will be late.
Quick Answer
  • Voy a llegar tarde porque me desperté tarde y yo no pude encontrar las llaves de mi coche.

  • Llegaré tarde porque no estaba funcionando mi auto y yo estaba haciendo mi tarea de clase este mañana.

Let's take a look at another example:

Your friend’s birthday is coming up. Write the text message you send him with well wishes.

In this example, you are required to

  • Acknowledge that it is your friend's birthday,
  • Wish him well wishes.
Quick Answer
  • Feliz cumpleaños, Juan, y espero que tengas un buen día.
NB: You will be penalized if you write more than one sentence. Also, it is important to take note of the verb tense and phrases required for each scenario.

1. Keep Your Responses Short and Concise

When it comes to communication, sometimes less is more. Sentence structure cannot be emphasized enough; short, concise and simple sentences convey your ideas faster whereas complex sentences may make your response unclear an you risk making grammar erros and losing marks.

Your aunt sent you an email inviting you to a concert. Write a note declining the invitation.

Don't say
Mis padres van a celebrar su aniversario y voy a tener que quedarme en la casa para cuidar a mis hermanos así que no puedo ir.
Say
No puedo ir porque tengo que cuidar a mis hermanos.
