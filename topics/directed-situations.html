Directed Situations

  • Overview
  • Examples
  • Exercises
  • Resources

Directed Situations are short situations/ scenarios in which you are required to give a one sentence response in Spanish.

For example, you are given the following scenario:

  • Your teacher is taking up a new job overseas. Write him/ her a farewell note and express good wishes.

In this example, you are required to

  • Acknowledge that your teacher is leaving, and
  • Write him/ her a farewell note.

Suggested responses may be:

  • Good luck with your new job and I hope you do well.

    Why did we use 'usted' and not 'tÃº' ?

    They both means 'you', but the significance difference between them is that 'usted' is the polite form and 'tÃº' is informal, similarly to what you would use with your friends.

    Use 'usted' when you would like to:

    • People whom you wish to show respect.
    • Strangers.
    • An honorable figure or an authority.
    • A public audience or crowds
    • An older person

  • Congrats on your new job and I am going to miss you.
